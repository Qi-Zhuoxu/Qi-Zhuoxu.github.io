This paper uses Weibo image-text data, semantic segmentation, LightGBM, SHAP, and mediation analysis to investigate how landscape elements and sentiment jointly shape urban visual landscape perception on Xiamen Island. It identifies nonlinear thresholds and interaction effects among visual elements and shows that sentiment significantly mediates landscape preference, offering evidence-based guidance for designing urban environments that are both visually appealing and supportive of positive sentiment.
